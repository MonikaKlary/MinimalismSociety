Our Story

The Minimalism Society was founded in 2017 by a group of students from Robert Gordon University; Monika Klary, Paige MacLellan, Siddharth Kenia, Sean Bisset, Calum Smyth and Natalie Burns. The aim of this society is to introduce people to this amazing minimalist lifestyle, by coming together to discuss ideas and plans they have in this exciting subject. We encourage everyone to attend this society to see what minimalism could mean for them, whether they have been a minimalist for years, or are just curious to find out about the benefits and perks. This society not only offers to teach you the knowledge for you to save money, but also offers a wide range of activities. From helping in projects, to volunteering with different charities, we really do have different opportunities to cater to everyones taste. In our weekly meetings, we provide dominoes pizza and other snacks to cater for all tastes and diets. We also offer one-on-one meetings for the people who aren't comfortable in a group setting. We carry out different tasks and projects within the community to raise money for local charities and highly encourage everyone to donate clothes and other goods to charity shops. As a society, we look at different ways you can do more with less and show how you can use the same items different ways to save time and money. As a society, we also go on weekly social outings to places such as JamJar and ATIK to bond with each other and have a good time, to help to build new friendships within and outwith the society. At the end of every year, we have an annual society ball, where we encourage you all to dress up for one of the best nights of your life. We encourage everyone to try to attend this society to see how it can change your life for the better - you wont regret it! We meet at Robert Gordon University, in the Sir Ian Woods Building (room N117) every Wednesday. We look forward to meeting everyone at our next meeting!
